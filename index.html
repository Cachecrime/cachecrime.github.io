Mission

We exist because claims about Africa — its wars, its politics, its disasters — travel faster than the evidence behind them. Cachecrime builds the evidence back in, and trains the next generation of reporters to do it themselves.

Every investigation is published with its sourcing intact: the imagery, the metadata, the chain of verification. If we can't show our work, we don't publish.

Capabilities

Six disciplines, one evidence standard.

01 · RESEARCH

OSINT Investigations

Tracing actors, networks, and incidents through open-source signals — social media, satellite imagery, procurement records, and public filings.

02 · IMAGERY

Visual Investigations

Reconstructing events frame by frame from user-generated footage, satellite passes, and geolocation — built for scrollytelling and broadcast alike.

03 · FORENSICS

Forensic Verification

Authenticating footage and imagery — detecting reused, mislabeled, or AI-generated media before it shapes the public record.

04 · DATA

Data & Mapping

Structuring messy public records — court filings, incident logs, judicial activity — into datasets that hold up under scrutiny.

05 · ACCOUNTABILITY

Fact-Checking

Debunking viral claims at speed, with sourcing shown, not just asserted — from misused footage to fabricated official notices.

06 · CAPACITY

Training & Capacity-Building

Teaching OSINT and verification methods to newsrooms and independent reporters across the continent, so the skill doesn't sit with one team.

Method

How a claim becomes a verified finding.

Source

Collect the original claim, footage, or document — and everything attached to it.

Verify

Check authenticity: reverse image search, metadata, frame analysis, AI-detection tools.

Geolocate

Match terrain, structures, and shadows against satellite and mapping data.

Corroborate

Cross-reference independent sources until the finding stands on its own.

Publish

Release the finding with the evidence trail attached, not hidden behind it.
